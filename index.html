COMP47250 P7 — Explainable Financial Forecasting Platform

Updated June 24, 2026  ·  Interim demo completed June 23  ·  Final deadline August 18, 2026

Overview
A 6-person MSc project at UCD building an end-to-end platform that predicts 1 to 5-day returns for large-cap equities by fusing price, sentiment, and macro signals into three ML models.
Objectives
Collect and align four signal types, train and compare XGBoost, LSTM, and TFT, apply SHAP explainability to every prediction, and backtest with realistic transaction costs to prove economic value.
OHLCV Technicals Sentiment Macro
Success metric
Sharpe ratio above 0.8 beating the market index benchmark, with all three models live, SHAP explanations visible per prediction, and a deployed public dashboard.

What the end product looks like
  • A live public dashboard showing today's BUY / SELL signal for each stock, updated every 3 hours via a cron service running on Render.
  • Three trained models — XGBoost, LSTM, and TFT — each producing a confidence score, with their results compared side by side on accuracy and financial KPIs.
  • A SHAP explanation panel on every prediction page showing exactly which signals (RSI, sentiment, MACD, etc.) drove the forecast and by how much.
  • A walk-forward backtest report with Sharpe ratio, maximum drawdown, and Calmar ratio compared against a buy-and-hold benchmark.
  • A REST API backed by Supabase (PostgreSQL) serving all predictions, sentiment scores, and backtest results to the frontend.
Interim demo completed June 23. The team presented a working pipeline, XGBoost model, live frontend, and cron service. Next focus is closing the three gaps before the final demo on August 3.
