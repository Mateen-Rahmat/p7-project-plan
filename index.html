COMP47250 P7 - Explainable Financial Forecasting Platform

Updated June 24, 2026  ·  Interim demo done June 23  ·  Final deadline August 18, 2026

Overview
Six MSc students at UCD building a platform that predicts short-term stock returns by combining price data, news sentiment, Reddit activity, and macro indicators into three machine learning models.
Objectives
Collect and clean four types of signals, train and compare XGBoost, LSTM, and TFT models, use SHAP to explain every prediction, and run a realistic backtest that accounts for transaction costs.
OHLCV Technicals Sentiment Macro
Success metric
A Sharpe ratio above 0.8 that beats the market benchmark, all three models running live, SHAP explanations visible for each prediction, and the whole thing deployed as a public dashboard.

What the finished platform will look like
  • A live dashboard showing today's buy or sell signal for each stock, refreshed every 3 hours by a background service running on Render.
  • Three trained models (XGBoost, LSTM, and TFT) each producing a confidence score, with results displayed side by side so you can compare how they perform.
  • A SHAP explanation panel on every stock page showing which signals actually drove the prediction and by how much, so the output is never just a black box.
  • A backtest report with Sharpe ratio, maximum drawdown, and Calmar ratio, all measured against a simple buy-and-hold strategy so the results are honest and comparable.
  • A REST API backed by Supabase that serves predictions, sentiment scores, and backtest results to the frontend in real time.
The team presented the interim demo on June 23 and showed a working data pipeline, a trained XGBoost model, a live frontend, and the cron service running in production. The next focus is closing three gaps before the final demo on August 3.
